An Indian Air Force Tejas fighter jet crashed during a flying display at the Dubai Airshow 2025 on Friday (November 21, 2025) claiming the life of the pilot. The accident occurred at approximately 2.15 p.m. local time at Al Makhtoum International Airport, which is the venue for the Airshow. Viral visuals on social media capture the aircraft spiraling out of control just above the ground, igniting on impact, and sliding for a short distance before being engulfed in flames.

Advertisement “The pilot sustained fatal injuries in the accident,” the Indian Air Force said in a statement and announced constitution of a court of inquiry to ascertain the cause of the accident. Dubai’s media office said that firefighting and emergency teams responded rapidly to the incident and were currently managing the situation on-site. Friday (November 21) was the last day of the Airshow which opened on November 17. Besides the global aviation industry that congregates at the largest such event in the region, the airshow is open to the public for aerial and static displays of aircraft which include commercial and military jets to private aircraft, UAVs, drones, and helicopters
