RBI,European Central Bank agree to start realisation phase for UPI-TIPS link

The RBI and NIPL will continue to collaborate closely with European Central Bank to operationalize the UPI–TIPS link, including technical integration, risk management and settlement arrangements

Reserve Bank of India (RBI) and NPCI International Payments Ltd (NIPL) on one hand and European Central Bank on the other, to connect Unified Payments Interface (UPI) with the TARGET Instant Payment Settlement (TIPS), the instant payment system operated by the Eurosystem, have agreed to start the realisation phase for the UPI–TIPS.

The RBI and NPCI International Payments (NIPL) have been engaging with European Central Bank on the initiative to connect UPI with the Target Instant Payment Settlement, the instant payment system operated by the Eurosystem. Following constructive and sustained engagement, both sides have agreed to start the realisation phase for the UPI-TIPS link, the RBI said today.

The UPI is currently available in seven countries -- Bhutan, France, Mauritius, Nepal, Singapore, Sri Lanka and the UAE. This allows Indians to make payments while traveling or living in these countries, often through partnerships with local payment systems.
